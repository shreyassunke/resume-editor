# V3 — VC / Startup / Generalist

## Purpose

For venture and early-stage roles where judgment, ownership, and a builder's instinct
matter more than algorithmic depth. The framing is operator-first: ATLAS reads as a
venture you are building, ProfessorAI as a founding story, and HUMN AI as the place you
made business-level architecture and growth bets.

Note: VC recruiting is relationship-driven. Your pitch and network matter more than this
document, so perfect V1 and V2 first and treat this as supporting material.

## Targets

- **Roles:** VC Intern, Startup Generalist, Product + Engineering hybrid, Founding
  Engineer Intern
- **Companies:** Madrona, Flying Fish, Series A/B startups, accelerator programs

## ATS keywords this version leads with

founder, 0-to-1, product strategy, go-to-market, growth, business model, fundraising,
due diligence

## Bullet-ordering rationale

- **HUMN AI** is reframed in operator language: technical-direction ownership first,
  then GTM traction (vendor/listing growth as the metric), then revenue infrastructure
  (Stripe), then the 30% cost cut framed as extending runway.
- **ATLAS** leads with the founding/0-to-1 narrative (problem identified, decision to
  build solo), then user value with the jargon trimmed, then product roadmap as
  vertical expansion strategy.
- **ProfessorAI** is expanded to two bullets to surface the from-zero founding story
  and the GTM/business-model angle.
- **Skills** foreground Product Strategy, 0-to-1 Development, Go-to-Market, and Business
  Modeling. Range over depth.
